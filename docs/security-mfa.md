# Two-factor authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication adds a second step to your login: your password **plus**
a 6-digit code from an app on your phone. Even if someone learns your password,
they can't get in without your phone. We recommend it for every admin and
committee account.

## Turning it on

1. Go to **Account → Security**.
2. Tap **Set up two-factor**.
3. On your phone, open an authenticator app — **Google Authenticator**, **Authy**,
   **Microsoft Authenticator** or a password manager like **1Password**.
4. Scan the QR code (or type the key shown underneath it).
5. Enter the 6-digit code your app displays, then tap **Verify & turn on**.
6. **Save your backup codes** — copy or download them and keep them somewhere safe.

That's it. Next time you log in, after your password you'll be asked for the
current 6-digit code from your app.

## Backup codes

When you turn 2FA on we give you a set of one-time **backup codes**. If you ever
lose your phone, a backup code lets you get back in. Each code works once. You can
generate a fresh set anytime from **Account → Security** (which replaces the old
ones).

## If you lose your phone

You have two options:

- **Use a backup code** on the login screen, then set 2FA up again on your new
  phone.
- **Ask a club admin** (or SportsWeb One support) to reset your 2FA. After that you
  can log in with your password and set it up again.

## For admins: resetting someone's 2FA

A senior admin can clear a member's 2FA if they're locked out — the member then
logs in with their password and sets it up again. (This uses a secure reset that
your club admin can run; SportsWeb support can also help.)

## Good to know

- 2FA protects your **login**, not a specific device — set it up once and it
  applies wherever you sign in.
- Turning 2FA off is in the same **Account → Security** panel.
- The per-login code step only appears once 2FA is on for your account.
